Meira rode with Amelia in her carriage, where Amelia spent a good portion of time squealing so<br>
gleefully that the entire caravan could hear. I tried to maneuver close enough to listen in, but she<br>
shooed me away, claiming her god-given right to private conversation.#
*
“Countess Amelia?” I spoke uncertainly at her door.<br>
“Yes?” She replied poking her head out to see who it was. Her hair was sticking out in all directions as if she had scrubbed it and not brushed it out.#
*
“It’s difficult to be single in this court. The married women think you’re after their husbands and so do not trust you, and the men think that unmarried women have nothing of substance to say.”#
